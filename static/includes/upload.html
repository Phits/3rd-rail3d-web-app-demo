For a Railer beginner, or someone with experience, the process may start something like this:

• “The Railer will provide a 2D drawing or rendering of the product to be prototyped and printed – this need not be a highly detailed schematic or work of art, merely something sketched-out and comprehensible…that’s all you need!”
• “The rendering shall then be scanned and previewed in a program like Adobe Photoshop where the object will be “cleaned-up” by filling-in gaps in the drawing utilizing the palette toolbar in Photoshop” • “This may take a little time but attention to detail is key to getting the rendering fully prepped for the next step” • “The cleaned-up Photoshop image can then be dragged into Adobe Illustrator so the image can be vectorized”
